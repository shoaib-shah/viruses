Human Herpesviruses

Human herpesviruses (HHVs) are a group of eight viruses that can cause a variety of diseases in humans. These include well-known infections such as cold sores, genital herpes, chickenpox, and mononucleosis.

Types of Human Herpesviruses

  • HHV-1 (Herpes Simplex Virus 1 - HSV-1): Causes oral herpes (cold sores) and can also cause genital herpes.
  • HHV-2 (Herpes Simplex Virus 2 - HSV-2): Primarily causes genital herpes.
  • HHV-3 (Varicella-Zoster Virus - VZV): Causes chickenpox and shingles.
  • HHV-4 (Epstein-Barr Virus - EBV): Associated with infectious mononucleosis and certain cancers.
  • HHV-5 (Cytomegalovirus - CMV): Can cause serious complications in immunocompromised individuals.
  • HHV-6 (HHV-6A and HHV-6B): Associated with roseola infantum.
  • HHV-7: Similar to HHV-6, often asymptomatic.
  • HHV-8 (Kaposi's Sarcoma-associated Herpesvirus - KSHV): Linked to Kaposi's sarcoma and certain lymphomas.

Transmission and Infection

Human herpesviruses are transmitted through direct contact with infected bodily fluids. After initial infection, the virus can establish latency in the body and may reactivate later.

Clinical Manifestations

Common diseases caused by human herpesviruses include:

  • Cold Sores
  • Genital Herpes
  • Chickenpox
  • Shingles
  • Infectious Mononucleosis

Diagnosis and Treatment

Diagnosis is typically based on clinical evaluation and laboratory tests. Antiviral medications can help manage symptoms and reduce the frequency of outbreaks.

Prevention

Preventive measures include avoiding direct contact with active lesions and practicing safe sexual habits.
