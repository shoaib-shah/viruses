What is Coronavirus (COVID-19)?

Coronavirus disease (COVID-19) is an infectious disease caused by a newly discovered coronavirus. Most people infected with the COVID-19 virus will experience mild to moderate respiratory illness and recover without requiring special treatment. However, older people and those with underlying medical problems like cardiovascular disease, diabetes, chronic respiratory disease, and cancer are more likely to develop serious illness.

Breaking News: COVID-19 Vaccine Distribution

Several vaccines have been approved for emergency use, and the global rollout is now underway. Health authorities are focusing on vaccinating the most vulnerable populations first, followed by the general public. These vaccines have shown promise in preventing severe illness and reducing the spread of the virus.

COVID-19 Variants and Their Impact

The emergence of new variants of the coronavirus has raised concerns globally. Experts continue to monitor the variants to understand their transmissibility, severity, and whether they can evade immunity from past infections or vaccinations.

Prevention Tips to Stay Safe

  • Wash your hands frequently with soap and water for at least 20 seconds.
  • Avoid close contact with people who are sick.
  • Wear a mask in crowded places and practice physical distancing.
  • Stay home when you feel unwell, and get tested if you show symptoms.
  • Clean and disinfect frequently touched objects and surfaces regularly.
  • Cover your mouth and nose with a tissue or elbow when you cough or sneeze.
  • Get vaccinated when eligible to protect yourself and others.
