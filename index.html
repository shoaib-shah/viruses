Viruses And Disease

A virus is a microscopic infectious agent that replicates only inside the living cells of an organism. Viruses can infect all types of life forms, including animals, plants, fungi, and even microorganisms like bacteria (in which case they are called bacteriophages).

A virus is a tiny infectious agent that can only replicate inside a living host cell. It consists of genetic material (DNA or RNA) enclosed in a protein coat, and sometimes a lipid envelope. Viruses can infect animals, plants, and microorganisms, causing diseases like the flu, COVID-19, and HIV. They spread through various means, such as contact, air, or vectors like mosquitoes. While harmful, viruses are also studied for beneficial uses, such as in vaccines, gene therapy, and phage therapy.

The Role of Viruses in Science and Medicine

A virus is a microscopic infectious agent that relies on living host cells to replicate. It consists of genetic material (DNA or RNA) enclosed in a protein coat, and sometimes a lipid envelope. Viruses can infect all forms of life, including humans, animals, plants, and microorganisms, and they are responsible for diseases like the flu, HIV/AIDS, and COVID-19. While harmful in many cases, viruses are also being studied for beneficial applications, such as in gene therapy and phage therapy.
