Year-Wise Virus Listing

1933
Influenza Virus
Influenza is a highly contagious viral infection of the respiratory tract that causes fever, cough, sore throat, body aches, and fatigue. It can cause seasonal epidemics.
2019
Coronavirus (COVID-19)
COVID-19 is caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus, a highly contagious respiratory illness that emerged in 2019. Symptoms range from mild respiratory distress to severe pneumonia.
1950s
Enteroviruses
Enteroviruses primarily infect the gastrointestinal tract and can cause diseases like hand, foot, and mouth disease, and in severe cases, meningitis or polio.
1960s
Human Herpesviruses
This family includes viruses causing diseases such as cold sores (HSV-1), genital herpes (HSV-2), chickenpox (VZV), and mononucleosis (EBV).
1956
Human Papillomaviruses (HPV)
HPV causes warts, and certain types are linked to cancers such as cervical cancer. Vaccines are available to prevent infection by high-risk strains.
1940s
Flaviviruses (e.g., Dengue, Zika)
Flaviviruses are transmitted by mosquitoes and cause diseases such as dengue fever, Zika, yellow fever, and West Nile virus.
1796-1958
Orthopoxviruses (Smallpox, Monkeypox)
Orthopoxviruses include smallpox and monkeypox, causing skin lesions. Smallpox was eradicated through vaccination.
1970s
Hepatitis Viruses
Hepatitis viruses cause liver inflammation. Types A, B, C, D, and E are differentiated based on their transmission routes and severity.
1950s
Chickenpox (Varicella Zoster)
Varicella-zoster virus causes chickenpox, a highly contagious disease characterized by a blister-like rash, fever, and tiredness.
1950s
Common Cold (Rhinovirus)
Rhinoviruses cause the common cold, leading to symptoms such as a runny nose, sore throat, and cough.
1934
Mumps Virus
Mumps is a viral infection that affects the salivary glands, causing swelling and discomfort. It can lead to complications like meningitis.
1964
Epstein-Barr Virus (EBV)
EBV is associated with infectious mononucleosis and is linked to certain cancers like Burkitt lymphoma.
2003
Mimivirus
Mimivirus is a giant virus, one of the largest known, infecting amoebas, with a very large genome compared to typical viruses.
1967
Marburg Virus
Marburg virus causes hemorrhagic fever, similar to Ebola, with a high mortality rate and transmission through bodily fluids.
1915
Caudoviricetes (Bacteriophages)
Bacteriophages are viruses that infect bacteria. They are not harmful to humans but play a role in controlling bacterial populations.
1976
Ebola Virus
Ebola causes severe hemorrhagic fever, transmitted through direct contact with infected bodily fluids, often with high mortality.
1909
Orthobornavirus (Borna Disease Virus)
Borna disease virus causes neurological diseases in animals, particularly horses and birds, and may have implications for human health.
1967-1976
Filoviridae (Ebola, Marburg)
Filoviruses, such as Ebola and Marburg, cause hemorrhagic fevers with high fatality rates, transmitted through direct contact with bodily fluids.
1970s
Caliciviridae (Norovirus)
Caliciviruses, including Norovirus, are responsible for outbreaks of gastroenteritis, leading to symptoms like vomiting, diarrhea, and stomach cramps.
1930s
Orthomyxoviridae (Influenza)
Orthomyxoviruses include influenza, a viral infection causing fever, cough, body aches, and fatigue. It mutates rapidly and causes seasonal epidemics.
