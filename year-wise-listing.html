Year-wise Virus Listing

Year Virus Name Description Image
1930 Yellow Fever Virus A viral disease transmitted by mosquitoes, causing fever and jaundice.
1956 Poliovirus Causes poliomyelitis, leading to paralysis in severe cases.
1967 Marburg Virus A rare but deadly virus that causes hemorrhagic fever.
1976 Ebola Virus A highly contagious virus causing severe hemorrhagic fever with high mortality.
1983 HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus) Causes AIDS, weakening the immune system over time.
1989 Hantavirus A virus spread by rodents, causing severe respiratory issues in humans.
2000 West Nile Virus Primarily transmitted by mosquitoes, leading to neurological diseases.
2002 SARS-CoV Causative agent of Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome, transmitted via respiratory droplets.
2009 H1N1 Influenza (Swine Flu) A type of influenza virus that caused a global pandemic.
2012 MERS-CoV Causative agent of Middle East Respiratory Syndrome, related to SARS-CoV.
2013 Chikungunya Virus A mosquito-borne virus causing fever and joint pain.
2019 SARS-CoV-2 (COVID-19) Causes COVID-19, a respiratory illness leading to a global pandemic.
2005 Avian Influenza Virus A virus affecting birds, with the potential to mutate and affect humans.
1923 Rabies Virus A fatal viral infection affecting the brain, usually transmitted by animal bites.
1947 Dengue Virus A mosquito-borne virus causing high fever, rash, and severe muscle and joint pain.
1959 Rotavirus Common cause of severe diarrhea in infants and young children.
1964 Herpes Simplex Virus A virus causing oral and genital herpes.
2004 Monkeypox Virus A rare viral disease similar to smallpox, often transmitted from animals.
2007 Human Metapneumovirus Causes respiratory infections in infants, the elderly, and immunocompromised individuals.
2018 Zika Virus Primarily transmitted by mosquitoes, associated with birth defects when pregnant women are infected.
2015 Enterovirus D68 Causes respiratory illness, especially in children, leading to wheezing and difficulty breathing.
1973 Hepatitis B Virus Causes chronic liver disease and can lead to liver cancer.
1986 HIV-2 A less common form of HIV, primarily found in West Africa.
1953 Adenovirus Causes respiratory infections, conjunctivitis, and gastrointestinal illness.
1972 Norovirus Causes acute gastroenteritis, leading to stomach cramps and vomiting.
1965 Herpes Zoster (Shingles) Causes a painful rash, typically appearing after a previous chickenpox infection.
1969 Lassa Virus A hemorrhagic fever virus transmitted by rodents, mainly in West Africa.
